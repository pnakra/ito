## Automatic Red Flag Triggers

The following patterns trigger an **immediate Red flag** regardless of any other context or AI interpretation:

### Incapacitation Indicators
- References to someone being passed out, asleep, or unconscious
- References to someone being "too drunk" or "too high" to respond
- Phrases like "won't remember"

### Force or Coercion Indicators
- Words like "force" or "forced"
- Phrases like "make her/him/them do/have/give"
- References to holding someone down
- Phrases like "can't say no" or "won't say no"

### Victim Blaming / Boundary Dismissal
- "she was asking for it"
- "no means yes"
- Phrases implying someone "owes" sexual activity

### Threats or Secrecy
- "no one will believe"
- "keep it/this secret"

## Yellow Flag Triggers (Concerning Attitudes)

These patterns escalate risk to Yellow (or higher when combined with physical momentum):

### Derogatory Labels
- Slut, whore, thot, skank, and similar terms

### Objectifying Assumptions
- Describing someone as "easy"
- "gets around"

### Entitlement
- "she/he owes me"
- "I deserve"
- "friend zoned"
- "nice guy" framing

### Dismissing Boundaries
- "playing hard to get"
- "leading me on"
- "teasing me"
- "she wants it" (without actual consent)

### Pressure/Coercion
- "just let me"
- "come on"
- "don't be like that"

## What This Tool Does NOT Do

1. **Does not provide legal advice** - This is not a substitute for understanding consent laws in your jurisdiction
2. **Does not grant permission** - Only the other person can consent
3. **Does not replace professional help** - If you're struggling, please speak with a counselor or trusted adult
4. **Does not track or identify users** - We don't know who you are

## Privacy Note

- No personal data is collected or stored long-term
- Input is processed for analysis and then discarded
- Session data can be cleared at any time using "Clear this session"
- Clearing only affects your local view; it does not delete device cache or data already transmitted for processing

## Updates

This algorithm may be updated to improve safety. The core principle remains: **when in doubt, the system errs toward caution.**
